About Me

I’ve always believed that learning isn’t a phase—it’s a way of life. From my early academic days to my professional journey, I’ve held myself to high standards, pushing forward to learn and achieve more. In my bachelor’s degree, I was proud to finish in the top 1% of my department, a reflection of my dedication to my craft. My drive led me to pursue a master’s, where I graduated with a perfect 4.0 GPA. For me, these accomplishments aren’t just about the scores; they represent my love for uncovering knowledge, solving complex problems, and mastering new challenges. Learning is my foundation, and it’s what keeps me motivated to grow every day.

Creating Real Impact

I’m passionate about building technology that improves lives in tangible ways. Working at ADP gave me the chance to develop key features for their WorkForceNow OnTheGo product, such as employee scheduling and role-based authorization, used by thousands of small businesses across Canada. I’ll never forget the feedback from small business owners who shared how these features simplified a time-consuming task and made employee management smoother. Knowing that something I created helped real people in their daily work is why I do what I do. It reminds me that engineering isn’t just about code—it’s about connection.

Designing for Scale

One of the most humbling experiences of my career was working with Kore.ai on Mashreq Bank's Bank Assist chatbot, which serves over half a million customers. Designing conversational flows that could effectively guide users through complex banking tasks was both challenging and rewarding. Collaborating with the bank to understand user needs and deliver an intuitive, reliable experience reminded me of technology’s incredible reach. The project reinforced my belief that great design isn’t just functional; it’s human-centered. Helping people navigate something as critical as banking with ease is a responsibility I cherish.

Mentorship and Collaboration

While I love the technical side of my work, I also find joy in supporting others on their journeys. At ADP, I had the opportunity to mentor two juniors, guiding them through our product’s architecture and design practices. Watching them grow from newcomers to confident contributors was incredibly rewarding. As a TA during my master’s, I introduced undergraduates from non-CS backgrounds to core concepts, helping them gain skills they could use in their careers. Whether it’s building solutions or mentoring others, my aim is always to make a meaningful impact on people’s lives.

Building Tomorrow’s Solutions

Looking to the future, my aspiration is to transition into a role where I can design and architect solutions that touch lives on a global scale. I want to move beyond just development into shaping meaningful experiences, creating technology that’s not only functional but truly transformative. My journey in self-driven projects and academic challenges has shown me the thrill of building from the ground up, and I am excited to one day design solutions that people everywhere will rely on and enjoy. For me, engineering is not just a job—it’s a way to connect, innovate, and empower.
